## General Online Safety Tips

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every online account.
   Avoid using your name, date of birth, or simple sequences like
   "123456". Use a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important
   accounts including email, social media, and banking apps. This
   adds an extra layer of security even if your password is stolen.

3. **Never click on suspicious links** received via SMS, WhatsApp,
   email, or social media — even if they appear to come from a
   known contact or a trusted brand. Always verify the URL before
   entering any details.

4. **Keep your apps and phone software updated** regularly.
   Updates often contain important security patches that protect
   against the latest cyber threats.

5. **Do not share personal information** such as your Aadhaar
   number, PAN card, date of birth, or home address on public
   platforms or with unknown individuals online.

6. **Be cautious on public Wi-Fi networks** — avoid logging into
   banking apps or entering passwords when connected to free/public
   Wi-Fi in cafes, malls, or college canteens. Use mobile data
   for sensitive transactions.

7. **Verify the identity** of anyone who contacts you claiming to
   be from a bank, government office, or company before sharing
   any information or clicking any link they send.

8. **Regularly check your bank statements and UPI transaction
   history** for any unauthorised or suspicious activity, and
   report it immediately if found.

## UPI & Financial Safety Tips (Specific to India)

9. **Remember: You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to RECEIVE
   money.** If anyone asks you to enter your PIN, scan a QR code,
   or approve a request to receive a payment — it is a FRAUD.
   Entering your UPI PIN always means money is going OUT.

10. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not
    even with someone claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay,
    PhonePe, or Paytm customer support. Legitimate companies will
    NEVER ask for this information.

11. **Double-check the UPI ID and phone number** before confirming
    any payment. Fraudsters often use UPI IDs that look very
    similar to legitimate ones (e.g., support@ybl instead of
    the real merchant ID).

12. **Set a transaction limit** on your UPI apps for daily
    transfers to minimise loss in case your phone or account is
    ever compromised.

## If You Are a Victim – What To Do

- **Act immediately** — call your bank's helpline to freeze your
  account or block the UPI transaction if possible.
- **Report the cybercrime** at the National Cyber Crime Reporting
  Portal: **cybercrime.gov.in**
- **Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** (available
  24x7) to report financial fraud and initiate recovery steps.
- Save all evidence — screenshots, transaction IDs, phone numbers,
  and chat messages — before reporting.
- File a complaint at your nearest police station under the
  IT Act, 2000.
