## What is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where
fraudsters trick individuals into transferring money or revealing
sensitive payment credentials through deception, impersonation, or
fake links. It is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India,
targeting millions of smartphone users every year.

---

## How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

1. **Contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call,
   WhatsApp, or SMS, posing as a bank official, customer support
   executive, or online marketplace buyer/seller.

2. **Deception:** They create a sense of urgency — claiming the
   victim's account will be blocked, or that they are sending a
   payment and the victim just needs to "approve" it.

3. **Fake Request:** The fraudster sends a COLLECT request on a UPI
   app (like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm) and convinces the victim to
   enter their UPI PIN to "receive" money — but entering the PIN
   actually authorises a payment OUT of the account.

4. **Data Theft (alternate method):** In some cases, the fraudster
   shares a phishing link that looks like a legitimate UPI or bank
   page, and steals the victim's UPI PIN, OTP, or bank credentials.

5. **Money Lost:** The moment the victim enters the PIN or OTP, the
   money is instantly debited and transferred to the fraudster's
   account — often across multiple mule accounts, making it very
   hard to trace.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time UPI users and senior citizens unfamiliar with digital
  payments
- College students selling/buying items on OLX or Facebook
  Marketplace
- Small business owners and shopkeepers using QR codes
- Anyone who has recently posted about a financial transaction
  publicly

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss — often ranging from a few hundred to
  several lakhs of rupees
- Emotional distress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty in recovering money as transactions are instant and
  irreversible
- Risk of identity theft if banking credentials are also stolen
- Legal complications if the victim's account is unknowingly used
  as a mule account

---

## Real Scenario Example

A first-year college student in Bhopal listed his old laptop for
sale on OLX. A buyer contacted him and said he would pay via UPI.
The buyer sent a "collect request" of ₹15,000 on PhonePe and told
the student to enter his PIN to accept the payment. The student
entered his PIN thinking he was receiving money — but instead,
₹15,000 was instantly debited from his account. The buyer's number
was switched off immediately after.
